##SCREENING FOR RED FLAGS (per eTG)
######
INFECTION:
-Fevers
-Immunosuppression
-Penetrating wounds
-IVDU 
-Known infective diseases

FRACTURE:
-Significant trauma
-Minor trauma in person aged
-Known osteoporosis
-Steroid use

MALIGNANCY:
-History of malignancy
-Age>50
-Failure to improve with treatment
-Night sweats 
-Fatigue
-Unexplained weight loss
-Pain at multiple sites
-Pain at rest
-Pain at night or waking at night 

VISCERAL DISEASE (EG. PANCREATITIS, AORTIC ANEURYSM):
-Sudden pain in abscess of trauma
-Absence of pain aggravated by movement
-Syncope or hypotension
-Abdominal pain

CAUDA EQUINA SYNDROME:
-Altered bladder and/or bowel function
-Saddle anaesthesia 
-Foot or leg weakness 

SPINAL CORD PATHOLOGY:
-Decreased sensation in lower limbs
-Weakness in lower limbs
-Difficulty walking
-Paraesthesia in lower limbs

ANKYLOSING SPONDYLITIS:
-Age<40
-Duration >3 months
-Prolonged morning stiffness
-Night pain
-Improvement with physical activity
-Failure to improve with rest
-Highly responsive to NSAIDs
